Facts about Mercury

1

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun.

2

It is the smallest planet and is made of rock.

3

It is so close to the Sun that it only takes 88 days for it to complete it's orbit and is much hotter than Earth.

Key Words

Sun - the star in our Solar System.

Orbit - the path that a planet takes around the Sun, or that a moon takes around a planet.

Facts about Venus

1

Venus is the hottest planet and has an average temperature of 460°C.

2

It takes 225 days to travel around the Sun.

3

Like Earth, Venus has an atmosphere (air) around it, but it is a lot thicker than Earth’s and Venus is permanently covered in clouds.

Key Words

Atmosphere - the layer of gas around a planet.

1

Mars is a little smaller than Earth, but a lot further out. It used to have an atmosphere like Earth and Venus, but it doesn’t have very much anymore.

Facts about Jupiter

1

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System. You could fit 1,321 Earths inside Jupiter.

2

It is made of gas and is one of the four ‘gas giants’.

3

Jupiter is five times as far from the Sun as the Earth is and takes almost 12 years to travel around the Sun.

Key Words

Solar System– the Sun and the collection of stars.

‘Gas giants’ - a large planet that has a solid core, but a very thick atmosphere. This means that most of the planet is made up of gas.
